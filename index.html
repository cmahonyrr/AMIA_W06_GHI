Identifying Solutions to Challenges to AI Deployment in Biomedical Informatics

Insights from the US and Beyond · CFIR-guided · Equity-focused

November 15, 2025 · 8:30 AM – 12:00 PM (EST) · Atlanta Marriott Marquis, Atlanta, GA

Explore the workshop

Overview

Artificial Intelligence (AI) offers transformative potential for biomedical informatics—improving diagnostics, personalizing treatment, and accelerating research. Yet real‑world deployment faces challenges such as algorithmic bias, data interoperability, ethical concerns, and scalability constraints—risks that may widen health disparities if unaddressed. This three‑hour interactive workshop, organized by the AMIA Global Health Informatics Working Group, uses the Consolidated Framework for Implementation Research (CFIR) to surface practical strategies from both high‑resource and LMIC contexts, culminating in a co‑created, generalizable framework for equitable AI implementation.

Audience: clinicians, informaticians, researchers, policymakers, and health IT professionals.

Educational Objectives

  • Analyze challenges and opportunities for AI in biomedical informatics across settings.
  • Apply CFIR to systematically assess and address implementation barriers.
  • Co‑create a set of actionable strategies adaptable to diverse healthcare environments.
  • Promote locally led, sustainable approaches that advance health equity.

Program & Schedule

0:00–0:50

Panel Presentations

Empirical case studies across CFIR domains: intervention, outer/inner settings, individuals, and process.

0:50–1:20

Poster Walk

Ongoing initiatives from US and LMIC settings; networking and discussion.

1:20–2:10

Breakout Groups

One CFIR domain per table; moderators aggregate strategies, success/Failure points, and feasibility.

2:10–3:00

Report Out & Synthesis

Cross‑domain comparison and consolidation into a generalizable implementation framework.

Breakout prompts by CFIR domain
  • Intervention Characteristics: Adaptability, complexity, evidence strength, bias mitigation.
  • Outer Setting: Regulatory environment, market dynamics, equity considerations.
  • Inner Setting: Infrastructure readiness, leadership, workflows, communication.
  • Individuals: Skills, beliefs, training, multi‑disciplinary collaboration.
  • Process: Planning, execution, monitoring, iterative improvement and scale‑up.
